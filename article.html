Building the RSA Algorithm in Python

1/15/2025

The RSA Algorithm is a asymmetric encryption technique that uses two keys. A private key and a public key. The first step in building the two keys is to get 2 prime numbers. Now these prime numbers are going to be pretty big because we have to make it secure so we want two prime numbers that are around 1000 bits long each. You then have to compute n and z. Now n is going to be p*q (your two prime numbers that you chose) and your z is going to be the "totient" of your n, or just n = (p-1)(q-1). N is going to be apart of your private and public key while z is a number that we compute from n that helps us pick the exponents correctly so that the encryption and decryption perfectly undo eachother. It just makes everything in your system consistent
